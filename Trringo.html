“Trrringo: Transforming the rental tractor business in rural India”

Client

Mahindra & Mahindra

Platform

Android Application

Role

Lead Designer and Researcher

THE PROJECT

The current tractor-implement rental business is limited to local ecosystems built on interpersonal relationships, there is a need to have a central aggregator for effective distribution of resources and lowering of the rental for the farmer. The client came up with a hub and spoke model to address this problem and we were tasked to design an app to connect the drivers and renters to the hub, that is available in native languages.

RESEARCH

After meeting the stakeholders and determining the core problem, we skimmed through research reports published on the Indian agricultural sector by organizations such as Food and Agricultural Organization (FAO), Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) etc. (Links mentioned at the end) and embarked on field research in a small village 1600 KM away in the state of Maharashtra.

We interviewed 3 farmers and 3 tractor drivers.

CONCEPT

The findings from field research dictated the design to be simple:

  1. Driver were familiar with ‘WhatsApp’.
  2. Map based UIs as used by cab aggregators will not make sense to navigate to farms.
  3. Only 32% of the drivers reported on-time.

WIREFRAME/PROTOTYPE

Based on limited user feedback and mapping of client’s requirements, we proceeded with concept 2. Considering the apps intended deployment environment: Bright sunlight, vibrating tractors and uneducated drivers, high contrast colors were chosen (http://jxnblk.com/colorable/demos/text/). VISUALS

A limited color palette was used to guide the user towards urgent and intended actions. CTAs were sized according to the task priority. Shades of red and greens were used to indicate the status and context.

CONCLUSION

The app is currently in closed alpha stage right now. Based on client feedback, we refined the main flow by removing non-essential elements.One of the lessons learned during the span of the project was the rapid penetration of the smartphones in the rural and remote parts of India. This upcoming segment requires interfaces which are less text driven.
